“We want our music to tell stories.”

November 2, 2019
By Bilyana Karamfilova


Tell me a bit about your background, how did you get involved with music?


My family are all musicians. I have four uncles who play professionally. When we were kids my sisters learned to play piano and me and my brother – guitar. I started when I was six or seven and since then I am into music.I had my first band and my first concert when I was 14.


Can you introduce “Odekinys” with few words? Why did you pick up this name and what is the meaning?


Me and Edgaras had two other projects together before “Odekinys” here in Aarhus. First one was “Focus” - quite acoustic. Me on the bass and Edgaras on the piano. Then few years ago we renamed the project to “Kompas” and added some rock elements We were quite big band - 6 people on the stage. We renamed the project to “Odekinys” 6 months ago and it sounds more acoustic again. We both left our jobs and now we have full time in the project because we want to last long. The name of the band is a combination of words – ode from Odense and kinys from traukinys - the Lithuanian word for train.


You are a songwriter; can you tell me more about how a song is created?


I just take my guitar and I start playing and I think “Oh, that sounds cool!” and then I start finding a melody with my voice and then I’m thinking about the lyrics.


Courtesy of: Alec Jeppesen


Can you describe the music you play?


The music is basically pop-rock. It’s with a lot of feelings, colours and emotions. The way I act on the stage is quite theatrical. Some songs are very soft and sad, some are very pop and happy. We want our music to tell stories not just random words.


In which language do you sing?


All our songs are in English apart from two. One is in Danish which we’ll release at the end of December and one in French which will release next year, in spring.


What kind of music did you listen to when you were younger?


Moriarty, Cocoon, Parasol. Mostly french pop and folk music.


What do you listen to now?


Muse, Foo Fighters, RHCP, Arctic Monkeys, Queen


Courtesy of: Alec Jeppesen


What are your plans for the rest of the year? Do you have any planned concerts?


In the beginning of December, we are going to release two songs and next year we will release 4 more. We’ll have our concert in January/February.
